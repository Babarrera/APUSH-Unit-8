Ideological Roots & Postwar Reality

Communism vs. Capitalism

Bolshevik Roots: Established 1917 (Lenin). Based on Marx & Engels (Communist Manifesto). Goals: Classless society, abolition of private property.

Post-WWII Tension: At the Yalta Conference (1945), Stalin promised free elections in Poland/Eastern Europe. He broke this to create a buffer zone against Germany (20M Soviet deaths).

Post-WWII Architecture

  • Bretton Woods (1944): Created IMF and World Bank to stabilize the global economy.
  • United Nations (1945): Established to prevent WWIII.
  • Iron Curtain (1946): Churchill's speech defining the seal between East/West.
02

The Containment Machine

NSC-68 (1950) Goals 1. Block Soviet expansion. 2. Expose falsities of Soviet pretensions. 3. Induce retraction of Soviet influence. 4. Foster seeds of destruction within the Soviet system.

The China "Loss"

Chiang Kai-shek (Nationalist) vs. Mao Zedong (Communist). Despite U.S. aid, Chiang's corrupt gov fell in 1949. Republicans blamed Truman for "losing China."

Strategic Tools

  • Truman Doctrine: Containment through financial aid ($400M to Greece/Turkey).
  • Marshall Plan: Rebuilding Western Europe to stop communism's appeal.
  • CIA Ops: 1953 Iran (Ajax), 1954 Guatemala (protecting United Fruit Co).

Korea (1950-53)

Divided at 38th Parallel. First "Police Action" (no declaration of war). Proved containment could work but cost 33K+ American lives.

03

Domestic Politics & Fear

The 1948 Upset

Truman vs. Dewey: Everyone predicted a Dewey win. Truman won 303-189 electoral votes, fueled by his whistle-stop tour and civil rights stance.

The Republican Response

  • Taft-Hartley Act (1947): Outlawed closed shops, required anti-communist oaths from union leaders. Passed over Truman's veto.
  • 22nd Amendment: Limited presidents to 2 terms (repudiation of FDR).
  • McCarthyism: Reckless accusations. Media like I Led Three Lives and The Red Menace fueled the fire.

Eisenhower's "Hidden Hand"

Ike played the "grandfather" but was a master of covert power. He expanded Social Security and created the Interstate Highway Act (1956), yet warned of the Military-Industrial Complex in his 1961 farewell.

04

The Long Struggle for Equality

The NAACP Strategy Instead of K-12, the NAACP first targeted graduate and professional schools (like law schools) where the lack of "equality" was most legally undeniable.

Early Moves

  • Double-V: WWII slogan—Victory abroad/at home.
  • A. Philip Randolph: Pressured Truman to end military segregation (EO 9981).
  • Jackie Robinson: Broken barrier April 15, 1947.

Southern Resistance

Southern Manifesto: 96 congressmen signed a protest against Brown. Prince Edward County, VA: Closed its entire public school system (1959-64) rather than integrate.

Student Power

Greensboro Sit-ins (1960): Sparked 70,000+ participants nationwide. Freedom Rides: Tested interstate bus desegregation; met with mob violence.

The 1968 Breaking Point & Watergate

Election of 1968 Stats

  • Nixon (R): 301 EV (43.4% popular) — "Law and Order"
  • Humphrey (D): 191 EV (42.7% popular) — Hurt by Chicago riots.
  • Wallace (I): 46 EV (13.5% popular) — Segregationist "backlash."

The Nixon Fall

Watergate: Nixon didn't order the break-in, but he orchestrated the cover-up. Faced with certain impeachment, he resigned Aug 1974. War Powers Act (1973) limited the president's ability to wage war without Congress.

Exam Prep Q&A

What was the "hidden" missile trade of 1962?
To end the Cuban Missile Crisis, JFK publicly promised not to invade Cuba, but secretly agreed to remove U.S. missiles from Turkey and Italy. Soviet officer Vasili Arkhipov is credited with refusing to authorize a nuclear torpedo launch during the crisis, preventing total war.
How did the GOP respond to FDR's legacy?
They passed the 22nd Amendment to limit terms and the Taft-Hartley Act to roll back the power of labor unions granted by the Wagner Act.
What defined the Carter/Ford era?
Ford: Nixon's pardon (lost him support) and the Fall of Saigon. Carter: Camp David Accords (High point) vs. Iranian Hostage Crisis and Stagflation (Low points).
