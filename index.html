Harry Truman — Domestic Struggles & Fair Deal

22nd Amendment (1951) limited presidents to two terms — symbolic rebuke of FDR. Taft-Hartley Act (1947) over Truman’s veto: outlawed closed shop, banned secondary boycotts, required anti-communist affidavits. Union power gutted after Wagner Act. Fair Deal (1949) sought national health insurance, civil rights, education aid — blocked by Southern Democrats & Republicans. Only min wage raised (40¢→75¢). Yet Truman desegregated military via EO 9981 (1948) after pressure from A. Philip Randolph. First desegregated war: Korea.

Origins of the Cold War

Why begin? U.S. needed open markets in Europe/Asia; USSR wanted buffer zone in Eastern Europe. Stalin broke Yalta pledges. Pre-existing tensions: U.S. didn’t recognize USSR until 1933, Hitler-Stalin Pact (1939). “Cold” = no direct U.S.-USSR combat, but proxy wars (Korea, Vietnam).
George Kennan’s Long Telegram (1946) → Containment. Churchill’s “Iron Curtain” speech (March 1946). China falls to Mao (1949) globalized the threat.

Key Cold War Policies

Truman Doctrine (1947): $400M aid to Greece/Turkey — first containment application. Domino Theory logic.
Marshall Plan (1948-52): $12+ billion rebuilt Western Europe, rejected by Soviets; huge success.
Berlin Airlift (1948-49): Soviets blockaded West Berlin; U.S./Britain flew supplies 11 months → blockade lifted May 1949.

NATO (1949): attack on one = attack on all → Warsaw Pact (1955). NSC-68 (1950): massive military buildup (20% GNP → defense budget quadrupled). Goals: block Soviet expansion, expose falsities, rollback.
MAD (Mutually Assured Destruction): nuclear stockpiles prevented direct war. “Greatest existential threat is ourselves.”

Covert CIA ops under Eisenhower

  • Iran (1953 – Operation Ajax): overthrew Mossadegh (nationalized oil), installed Shah → long-term anti‑American sentiment.
  • Guatemala (1954): overthrew Arbenz (land reform threatening United Fruit). Set stage for civil war.
  • Indochina: funded France vs. Ho Chi Minh → Dien Bien Phu 1954, groundwork for Vietnam.

Eisenhower Presidency (1953–1961)

Hidden hand presidency – passive style, corporate cabinet, but accepted New Deal programs. New Look defense: nuclear “massive retaliation” over conventional forces.
Farewell Address (1961): warned of military‑industrial complex – alliance between defense industry and military that threatened democracy. Annual military spending > net income of all U.S. corporations combined.

🔴 Second Red Scare & McCarthyism

Sen. Joseph McCarthy (1950) claimed 205 Communists in gov’t – never evidence. “McCarthyism” = accusation without proof. Lost credibility in Army‑McCarthy hearings.
Hollywood Ten blacklisted. Margaret Chase Smith “Declaration of Conscience” (1950) denounced tactics. Smith Act, Internal Security Act, Federal Employee Loyalty Program. Rosenbergs executed (1953).
📺 Anti‑communist media: TV shows, films (I Married a Communist), comics. The Cincinnati Reds briefly renamed “Redlegs.” American Legion staged mock communist takeover in Mosinee, WI (1950).

🇰🇵 Korean War (1950–1953)

North Korea invaded South (June 1950). Truman called it UN “police action” – no declaration of war. MacArthur pushed north, China intervened. Truman fired MacArthur for insubordination (April 1951). Stalemate → armistice 1953: Korea split at 38th parallel, DMZ created. ~33,629 Americans killed. Effects: strengthened presidential war powers, deepened Cold War mentality, set stage for Vietnam.

Cuban Missile Crisis (1962)

Castro’s revolution (1959) → Bay of Pigs fiasco (1961). Soviets install nuclear missiles in Cuba. U.S. discovers Oct 15. Kennedy chooses naval quarantine instead of airstrike. Vasili Arkhipov (Soviet sub commander) refused to launch nuclear torpedo – saved world. Deal: U.S. secretly removed missiles from Turkey & promised not to invade Cuba; Soviets remove Cuban missiles. Closest to nuclear war in history.

🇻🇳 Vietnam War (1954–1975)

Domino Theory drove intervention. Gulf of Tonkin Resolution (1964) gave LBJ blank check – second attack never happened (government deception). Attrition & search‑and‑destroy → millions of refugees, Agent Orange.
Credibility gap – TV exposed brutality (CBS 1965). Tet Offensive (Jan 1968): military defeat for North but shattered U.S. public confidence. My Lai Massacre (1968) – 300–500 civilians killed; destroyed military trust.
Antiwar movement: SDS, teach-ins, draft resistance. Kent State (1970) – National Guard killed 4 students. Vietnamization (Nixon) shifted fighting to South. War Powers Act (1973) limited executive war power. Saigon fell 1975. 58,000 U.S. dead, $100B+. 26th Amendment lowered voting age to 18.

✊ Civil Rights Movement

Brown v. Board (1954) overturned Plessy – “separate inherently unequal.” Massive Resistance & Southern Manifesto. Emmett Till murder (1955) galvanized movement.
Montgomery Bus Boycott (381 days) – MLK emerges. Little Rock Nine – Eisenhower sent 101st Airborne to enforce integration. Sit‑ins (1960) → SNCC. Freedom Rides, Birmingham (police dogs & fire hoses), March on Washington (“I Have a Dream”). Civil Rights Act (1964) – banned discrimination in public accommodations & employment. Voting Rights Act (1965) – ended literacy tests, federal oversight. Loving v. Virginia (1967) – legalized interracial marriage.
Malcolm X (Black separatism/self‑defense) vs. MLK (nonviolence/integration). Black Power & Black Panthers (survival programs, police monitoring). MLK assassinated 1968.

Kennedy’s New Frontier & LBJ’s Great Society

JFK: New Frontier; Bay of Pigs; Cuban Missile; assassinated Nov 1963. LBJ: “War on Poverty” → Economic Opportunity Act (1964), Medicare/Medicaid (1965), HUD, Elementary & Secondary Education Act. However, Vietnam drained $21B/year, fueling inflation. Graphs from period show consistent racial gaps in life expectancy, poverty, infant mortality – legacy of Jim Crow.

1968: Year of Turmoil & Nixon Era

Tet Offensive, MLK & RFK assassinations, Chicago DNC “police riot.” Nixon wins with Southern Strategy appealing to white backlash.
Nixon: Vietnamization, détente, opened China (1972), SALT I, created EPA. Watergate – break‑in & cover‑up → resignation (Aug 1974). Only president to resign. Ford pardons Nixon.
Pentagon Papers revealed systematic government lying about Vietnam.

📢 Social Movements (1960s–1970s)

👩 Women’s Rights: The Feminine Mystique (1963) – Betty Friedan → NOW founded. Title IX (1972), Roe v. Wade (1973). ERA fails (Schlafly opposition).
🌱 Environmental: Rachel Carson Silent Spring (1962), Cuyahoga River fire (1969) → EPA (1970), Clean Air Act, Earth Day. Three Mile Island (1979).
🧑‍🌾 Latino Rights: César Chávez & Dolores Huerta – United Farm Workers; grape boycott (1965-70) wins wage increases. Brown Berets & Chicano walkouts.
🪶 American Indian Movement (AIM): Occupation of Alcatraz (1969), Trail of Broken Treaties, Wounded Knee (1973) → Indian Self‑Determination Act (1975).
🏳️‍🌈 Gay Liberation: Stonewall Inn uprising (1969) → Gay Liberation Front; first Pride parades (1970). APA declassified homosexuality as disorder (1973).

Postwar Economy & 1950s Mass Culture

GI Bill (1944) – college, home loans → middle class expansion. GDP doubled, baby boom (50 million births). Suburbanization: Levittown, FHA redlining, Interstate Highway Act (1956). Sun Belt growth. Television reaches 90% of homes; advertising & credit cards fuel consumerism. Rock ’n’ roll (Elvis, Chuck Berry) mixed racial boundaries.
Critics of conformity: Mills (The Power Elite), Riesman (The Lonely Crowd), Galbraith. Beat Generation (Kerouac, Ginsberg). 1960s counterculture: hippies, Woodstock (1969), SDS & Port Huron Statement – “participatory democracy.”

Rise of Modern Conservatism

Three goals: stop moral decline, limit federal gov, aggressive foreign policy. William F. Buckley (National Review). Goldwater (1964) championed states’ rights. Nixon’s Southern Strategy. 1970s evangelical surge – Moral Majority (Jerry Falwell), Focus on the Family. Laid foundation for Reagan Revolution.

📌 Exam Tips & Key Takeaways

  • Compare/Contrast: civil rights protests vs. anti-war protests; MLK/Malcolm X vs. earlier figures.
  • Brown v. Board overturned Plessy. Eisenhower enforced integration at Little Rock despite personal disagreement.
  • Gulf of Tonkin second attack never happened – led to credibility gap.
  • Containment = George Kennan’s idea; applied in Truman Doctrine, Korean War, Vietnam.
  • NSC-68 quadrupled defense spending & shaped Cold War militarization.
  • War Powers Act (1973) restored congressional checks after Vietnam.
  • 26th Amendment (1971): voting age 18 – “old enough to fight, old enough to vote.”
  • Cold War remained “cold” because superpowers never fought directly (proxy wars, MAD).
  • Know difference between JFK’s New Frontier and LBJ’s Great Society – LBJ passed landmark legislation.
  • For essays: connect WWII to postwar conditions (Cold War + civil rights gains).
